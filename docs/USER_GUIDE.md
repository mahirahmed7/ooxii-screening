# OOXii Screen — Volunteer User Guide

A quick guide to running a vision screening in the field. No internet is
needed once the app is installed — everything is stored on the phone and
exported as a CSV file at the end.

The whole screening takes about 2–3 minutes per person.

---

## Set up your profile (once)

Tap the **round profile icon in the top-right** of the home screen. Add your
**name** (and optionally a **photo**, **role**, and **organization**). The app
also assigns you a **staff/volunteer ID** automatically (e.g. `VOL-7Q3K9F`).
Your name and ID are stamped onto every exported CSV so the reporting team
knows who collected each screening. Tap **Save profile**.

---

## Before your first screening: calibrate the phone (once per device)

The app draws the eye-chart letters at a real physical size, so it needs to
know your screen's exact size. You do this **once** on each phone.

You will need: **any bank or ID card** (bank card, transit card, driver
licence — they are all the same standard size).

1. Open the app and tap **Calibration**.
2. **Stand the card on its end** against the **left edge** of the screen, so
   its long side runs top-to-bottom.
3. Use the **+1 / −1 / +10 / −10** buttons until the blue bar is exactly as
   tall as the card's long side — top and bottom lined up.
4. Tap **Save calibration**.

You can also set your testing distances here (see below). You only need to
redo calibration if you switch to a different phone.

> **Carry a measured distance aid.** The app assumes a fixed distance:
> a **3 m rope/string** for the distance test and a **40 cm string** for the
> near test. Cut them to length once and reuse them so every test is at the
> same distance. You can change these defaults on the Calibration screen.

## Running a screening

### 1. Start a new screening

From the home screen tap **➕ New screening** and fill in:

- **Year of birth**
- **Gender**
- **Cataract surgery** — right, left, both, or neither

Tap **Start distance test**.

### 2. Distance test (one eye at a time)

The patient stands at the measured **distance** (default 3 m) from the phone.

- The screen shows one eye at a time. **Cover the other eye** (the app tells
  you which one).
- A letter **E** in a square appears. It points up, down, left, or right.
- The **patient points** their hand in the direction the open side of the E
  faces. **You** tap the matching arrow (or swipe that direction).
- The **first E is practice** and is not scored.
- Repeat for the second eye when prompted.

The patient passes an eye if they read the line correctly (the app handles
the scoring — just enter what they signal).

### 3. Near test (both eyes)

The patient holds the phone at the measured **near distance** (default 40 cm),
**both eyes open, with their usual glasses OFF**.

- The letters get smaller as the patient answers correctly.
- The patient **swipes** the direction the E points (or you enter it for
  them).

### 4. What happens next depends on the result

- **Good distance + good near** → **PASS**. No further testing needed.
- **Good distance + poor near** → **Paddle test** (reading glasses). See below.
- **Poor distance in either eye** → **Refer for the full OOXii test**.

### 5. Paddle test (only if reading glasses are needed)

1. Let the client try the paddle and pick the **power** that works best
   (+1.0 to +3.0).
2. Tap the power, then **Glasses on — retest near vision**.
3. Dispense the reading glasses and **repeat the near test with the glasses
   on** to confirm they help.

### 6. Results

The results screen shows the outcome and the numbers recorded. The screening
is saved automatically to this phone.

## Exporting results

At the end of a session (or day), from the home screen tap **Export CSV**.
This opens the normal share sheet — email it, send it via a messaging app, or
save it to files. Send it to whoever runs the reporting pipeline.

Results stay on the phone until you delete the app, so you can export as often
as you like.

---

## Tips & troubleshooting

- **"Screen not calibrated" warning** — you must calibrate before testing, or
  the letters will be the wrong size. Tap Calibration and follow the steps.
- **Bright sunlight** — turn screen brightness to maximum.
- **Patient can't signal a direction** — the "first E is practice" step is
  there to teach them; don't score it. Demonstrate pointing/swiping first.
- **Wrong distance** — accuracy depends entirely on standing at the right
  distance. Always use the measured rope/string.
- **No internet in the field is fine** — the app is fully offline. You only
  need a connection to send the exported CSV, which can wait until later.

---

## A note on accuracy

This app reproduces a WHOEyes-style tumbling-E test but has **not** been
independently clinically validated. Treat it as a **screening** tool to sort
people quickly — anyone flagged for referral should get the full OOXii test.
